Value 01

Sincerity without performance.

No usernames you'd recognize, no public follower counts, no public likes to chase. When nobody's watching your profile, you can say the true, small thing: the smell of rain, your kid's terrible joke, the bus arriving on time. Strangers' glimmers hit different precisely because they have nothing to gain.

A gratitude journal that builds itself.

How It's Different
Give to get.

You can't lurk on My Glimmer. To see today's glimmers, you share one first. It sounds like a paywall; it's actually the point. The five seconds you spend finding your glimmer is the practice — everything after is the reward.

Strangers, on purpose.

Your group chat knows you. Your Instagram followers are keeping score. Glimmers from strangers carry none of that weight — no context, no comparison, no subtext. Just evidence that ordinary people everywhere are noticing beautiful things today, and you can too.

✦
One post type.

One per day. No stories vs. reels vs. threads. No optimal posting time. No decision fatigue. A glimmer is a sentence or two about something small and real. That constraint is what makes it a ritual instead of another feed.

100% human.

Every glimmer is written by a person about a moment that actually happened to them. In a feed increasingly full of generated everything, that's becoming the rarest thing online.
